## Overview

This document provides specifications and guidelines for capturing product images for the Steak Kenangan menu. These images will be used on the website, mobile app, and digital menu boards.

---

## General Requirements

### Technical Specifications

**Image Format**: JPEG
**Resolution**: Minimum 2000 x 2000 pixels (square format)
**Aspect Ratio**: 1:1 (square) preferred, 4:3 acceptable
**File Size**: 500KB - 2MB per image (optimized for web)
**Color Profile**: sRGB
**DPI**: 72 dpi (web), 300 dpi (print materials if needed)

### Quality Standards

- **Focus**: Sharp focus on main dish
- **Lighting**: Natural daylight or professional studio lighting
- **Background**: Clean, simple, non-distracting
- **Color**: Accurate color representation (no excessive filters)
- **Composition**: Follow rule of thirds
- **Steam/Freshness**: Capture steam for hot dishes to show freshness

---

## Photography Style Guide

### Brand Aesthetic
- **Mood**: Warm, appetizing, premium
- **Style**: Clean, modern, with Indonesian touches
- **Angle**: 45-degree angle (most flattering for food)
- **Distance**: Close enough to show detail, far enough to show full plate

### Lighting
- **Preferred**: Soft natural daylight from window
- **Alternative**: Professional softbox (5500K color temperature)
- **Avoid**: Direct harsh sunlight, yellow tungsten lighting
- **Direction**: Side lighting or 45-degree lighting

### Props & Styling
- **Plates**: Use actual restaurant plates (white porcelain for main courses)
- **Cutlery**: Stainless steel, clean, placed naturally
- **Garnish**: Fresh herbs, microgreens as actually served
- **Table**: Wooden table or neutral background
- **Napkins**: Linen napkins in earthy tones
- **Drinks**: Include relevant beverages in some shots

## Photography Checklist

### Pre-Production
- [ ] Confirm menu items list with kitchen
- [ ] Prepare all props and backgrounds
- [ ] Test lighting setup
- [ ] Confirm camera settings
- [ ] Have backup batteries and memory cards
- [ ] Coordinate with chef for food prep timing

### During Shoot
- [ ] Shoot multiple angles for each dish
- [ ] Capture RAW + JPEG if possible
- [ ] Check focus and exposure on camera LCD
- [ ] Take notes of any styling challenges
- [ ] Capture "making of" shots for social media

### Post-Production
- [ ] Color correction (accurate to real dish)
- [ ] Minimal retouching (remove dust, crumbs)
- [ ] Crop to specified aspect ratios
- [ ] Optimize file sizes for web
- [ ] Name files according to SKU
- [ ] Organize in folders by category

---

## File Naming Convention

Format: `{SKU}_{variant}_{angle}.jpg`

Examples:
- `WGY-RNDG_hero_45deg.jpg`
- `WGY-RNDG_fullplate_topview.jpg`
- `WGY-RNDG_detail_closeup.jpg`

**Variants**:
- `hero` - Main product shot
- `fullplate` - Complete plate with sides
- `detail` - Close-up of specific element
- `cutshot` - Cross-section view
- `lifestyle` - Action/interaction shot

**Angles**:
- `45deg` - 45-degree angle
- `topview` - Straight from above
- `sideview` - Straight from side
- `closeup` - Macro detail

## Don'ts (Common Mistakes to Avoid)

❌ Over-editing (unrealistic colors)
❌ Cluttered backgrounds
❌ Poor focus/blurry images
❌ Cold, lifeless food
❌ Inconsistent lighting across products
❌ Missing steam on hot dishes
❌ Wilted garnishes
❌ Dirty plates or cutlery
❌ Distracting shadows
❌ Wrong props (non-restaurant items)
